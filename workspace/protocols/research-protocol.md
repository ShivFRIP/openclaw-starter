# RESEARCH PROTOCOL — BINDING INSTRUCTIONS

**Status:** MANDATORY for ALL research outputs. No exceptions.
**Authority:** These rules override default LLM behaviour. Where a rule conflicts with your defaults, the rule prevails.
**Origin:** Compiled from 25 documented errors across the Judicial System Research Project. Every rule exists because a specific error was committed.

> At the START of every research session, confirm: "Research Protocol loaded. All 25 rules active."
> If the user identifies a deviation from any rule, course-correct IMMEDIATELY.

---

## A. SOURCE STANDARDS

### A1: Source-First, Never Inference-First
Every factual claim must be traceable to a specific source actually retrieved and read in this session. If a source does not say it, do not say it. No filling gaps with logical reasoning, no matter how reasonable.

If a claim cannot be backed by a source, state: **"I was unable to verify this from a primary source"** and flag the confidence gap.

> *Error that created this rule:* Saudi Arabia — two objection grounds ("underlying debt paid" and "security cheque") were logical deductions, not sourced, but presented alongside sourced grounds without distinction.

### A2: Primary Sources Over Secondary, Always
**Primary sources:** official government gazettes, court websites, statutory databases, judicial council publications, central bank directives, official legislation portals.

**Everything else is secondary:** law firm articles, academic papers, practitioner commentary, news reports.

Secondary sources can only serve as a pointer toward where to look. Every claim must then be verified against the primary official source. If only secondary is available, **label it explicitly as secondary**.

> *Error:* Bahrain Art. 393, Kuwait penalties, Jordan Art. 421, Turkey's procedural framework — all sourced from practitioner commentary alone without primary verification.

### A3: No Fabricated or Unverified URLs
Only provide a link if it has been actually fetched and confirmed to load the content described. If a URL cannot be accessed or verified, say so plainly.

### A4: Translate Non-English Primary Sources Rather Than Falling Back
Language is not an excuse to avoid primary sources. Search in the native language, fetch, translate, and verify. Do not report "cannot verify — language barrier" when native-language searches have not been attempted.

> *Error:* Multiple items reported as unverifiable due to language barriers. Iran Cheque Issuance Law was successfully retrieved in Farsi and translated. Turkey's Cheque Law verified from mevzuat.gov.tr in Turkish. Bahrain Art. 393 verified from sjc.bh in Arabic.

### A5: Current Law, Not Superseded Law
Always check whether the statute cited is the currently operative version. If conflicting versions are found, flag the discrepancy and ask before proceeding. Never cite a repealed or amended provision as current.

> *Error:* Nepal Section 107A — penalties cited as current after May 2025 repeal. Wrong thresholds, wrong imprisonment periods, wrong tier structure.

### A6: Never Replace Potentially Accurate Data Without Adequate Verification
If existing data says X, do not overwrite with Y from a secondary source without primary verification. Flag discrepancies transparently rather than silently picking one version.

> *Error:* Pakistan — "3 years + fine" (conjunctive) replaced with "3 years OR fine, OR both" (disjunctive) based on secondary sources without gazette verification.

### A7: Cross-Verify Key Claims Against At Least Two Independent Sources
Every key factual claim (penalty amounts, imprisonment terms, notice periods, section/article numbers) must be cross-verified against at least two independent sources.

> *Error:* Indonesia — "Section 492 KUHP" cited for fraud (actually public drunkenness). Correct: Article 378 KUHP (Penipuan/Fraud). Kuwait — "Decree 15/1978" cited; correct: Article 237, Penal Code Law 16/1960.

## B. CONTENT STANDARDS

### B1: Step-by-Step Detail, Not Surface-Level Summaries
Every procedure, sanction, or mechanism must be described step-by-step with the specific legal basis for each step. "Summary judgment available" is not acceptable. Full process: who files, where, what form, what fee, what timeline, what happens if contested.

### B2: Differentiate Government-Mandated vs Private/Institutional Sanctions
Clearly distinguish whether a sanction stems from government mandate (statute, regulation, central bank directive) or private bank policy. Standard banking practices are NOT government-mandated unless a specific regulation requires them.

> *Error:* Kuwait — "banks notify customers when cheques are dishonoured" listed as administrative sanction. This is standard banking practice.

### B3: Trace the Complete Information Flow
Trace: who originates → who transmits → who receives → what action is mandated → under what authority. Do not just state the endpoint.

### B4: Clarify When Parallel Procedures Apply
When listing multiple procedures, clearly state which scenario triggers which, and whether they run in parallel or are mutually exclusive.

> *Error:* UAE — three procedures listed without clarifying each applies to a different scenario (NSF → direct execution; account closure/stop payment → summary proceedings; fraud → criminal complaint).

### B5: Name Systems By Their Formal Name
When a formal administrative system exists, identify it by its official name. Do not describe generically when a specific name exists.

> *Error:* Malaysia — DCHEQS (Dishonoured Cheques Information System) not named; described generically.

### B6: Verify Whether a System Is Still Operational
Before describing any system, verify it is currently operational. If discontinued, state when it ended and what replaced it.

> *Error:* Malaysia DCHEQS described as BNM-administered after September 2020 discontinuation.

### B7: Never Conclude "No Legislation Exists" Without Exhaustive Search
A failure to find legislation is NOT the same as confirmed absence. Check official portals thoroughly including native-language searches.

> *Error:* Maldives — "No specific legislation" stated. Maldives has Negotiable Instruments Act (Law No. 16/95). Portal mvlaw.gov.mv not checked.

## C. DATA MANAGEMENT

### C1: Verify Column Headers Before Writing Any Data
Before writing any data, verify column headers. Read 2-3 existing populated rows to identify format. Match exactly.

### C2: Never Overwrite Existing Detailed Content Unless Correcting a Factual Error
If existing content is accurate, retain it. If replacing, explicitly highlight: what was changed, what the original said, why it was incorrect.

### C3: Compare Uploaded Files Against Prior Session Data
When a new file is uploaded, compare against prior session data. Flag data loss before proceeding.

### C4: Follow Established Column Format Consistently
Once a format is established, all subsequent rows must follow it identically.

## D. AUDIT AND DELIVERY

### D1: Pre-Delivery Self-Audit (MANDATORY CHECKLIST)
Before sending ANY research output, run through in order:
- [ ] Is every claim sourced?
- [ ] Is every source primary or clearly labelled secondary?
- [ ] Have all URLs been actually fetched and confirmed?
- [ ] Is the law cited the current operative version?
- [ ] Does the forum/procedure apply to the specific case type?
- [ ] Have any inferences been stated as fact?
- [ ] Are all columns correctly aligned with headers?
- [ ] Has any existing content been lost or overwritten?
- [ ] Are data gaps explicitly flagged?
- [ ] Is the confidence level stated?

### D2: Revision Before Delivery, Not After Challenge
Correct uncertainties BEFORE output reaches the user. Do not deliver confidently first and correct after being challenged.

### D3: Never Assume Accuracy of Own Prior Work
When re-verifying, check both content preservation AND factual accuracy. Do not assume prior outputs are correct.

### D4: State Confidence Level for Every Output
Every output must include: **HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW** with basis — what was verified from primary, what relied on secondary, what gaps remain.

### D5: Cite References in Every Output
Every factual claim attributed to specific source (document name, article number, URL if available).

### D6: State Data Gaps Explicitly
If information could not be found, say so explicitly. Never omit silently. State: **"This data point could not be verified from accessible sources in this session."**

### D7: Ask Before Assuming
When in doubt about scope, format, prioritisation, interpretation — ask. Do not assume.

## E. SEARCH METHODOLOGY

### E1: Official Portals First
Start every country's research with official government legislation portals and central bank websites. Only fall back to secondary after official portals checked.

### E2: Native-Language Search Is Default, Not Optional
For every country, conduct searches in the official language(s) in addition to English. Do not wait to be prompted.

### E3: Jurisdiction and Applicability Check
Verify that any forum suggested has subject-matter jurisdiction over the specific claim type. Do not extrapolate from general descriptions.

### E4: Present Discrepancies Transparently
When sources conflict, present both versions with sources. Flag for user to resolve. Do not silently pick one.
